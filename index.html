connecting curators to what really matters

Overview

Exhibits are supposed to tell stories and start conversations. Curators at the Williams College Museum of Art "…worry that people who are confused or frustrated aren’t expressing this," and wonder if they were able to “find pieces that invoke intriguing ideas and unexpected discussion points.”

Curators not only have limited feedback on visitor emotions, but even if they had a surplus of information, there would be no time in a curator’s busy schedule to sort through it. They have no good way to know whether exhibits (and specific pieces of art) cause confusion or spark inspiration in the visitors who experience them. Furthermore, curators have noticed that, due to their busy schedules, there’s a lack of connection between themselves and the museum visitors.

heART is designed to help curators communicate with visitors and understand their experiences.


We have created an application that allows curators to connect with visitors, and easily understand the vast amounts of data being collected by partner projects, WCMA staff, and pre-existing systems.
